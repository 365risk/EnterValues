Operational Risk

Operational risk is the risk of loss due to errors, interruptions, or damages caused by people, systems, or processes.

Introduction

Operational risk can affect an organization's ability to conduct business and achieve its objectives.

Types of Operational Risk

Operational risk includes:

  • Human Error: Mistakes made by employees or management.
  • System Failures: Disruptions caused by technology or system issues.
  • Process Failures: Breakdowns in business processes or procedures.

Assessment and Management

Managing operational risk involves:

  • Risk Assessment: Identifying and assessing potential operational risks.
  • Process Improvement: Enhancing processes to reduce the likelihood of failures.
  • Contingency Planning: Developing plans to address potential operational disruptions.

Example Scenario

An example of operational risk is a major IT system outage that affects business operations and customer service.

Mitigation Strategies

To mitigate operational risk, organizations can:

  • Implement Robust Controls: Establish controls to prevent and detect operational issues.
  • Enhance Training: Provide training to employees to reduce human error.
  • Monitor and Review: Continuously monitor and review operational processes and controls.

Conclusion

Managing operational risk is crucial for ensuring smooth business operations and minimizing disruptions. By implementing effective controls and contingency plans, organizations can better handle operational challenges.
